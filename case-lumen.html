the problem

Lumen was spending faster than it was earning. Marketing ops ran on manual handoffs — briefs in docs, campaigns built by hand, leads scored on gut — and blended CAC had climbed 28% in two quarters. Worse, nobody could say which channels actually produced pipeline: the website, the ad platforms, and the CRM each told a different story.

strategy

We treated CAC as an operations problem, not a media problem. Three pillars: install AI-driven content and ops workflows to kill the manual grind, rebuild the site around buyer intent instead of feature dumps, and fix attribution so every dirham of spend mapped to a closed-won record. Cheaper acquisition would come from doing more with the same budget — not from cutting it.

execution

We built AI workflows that turned product research into briefs, briefs into draft landing pages and ad variants, and inbound leads into scored, enriched CRM records — collapsing a multi-day cycle into hours. We rebuilt the marketing site on a modular system with intent-matched pages for each ICP segment, then rewired tracking with server-side events, UTM governance, and a CRM-to-ad-platform feedback loop. Bilingual Arabic/English variants shipped for GCC demand, and a self-serve reporting layer gave the team daily CAC-by-channel without waiting on an analyst.

creative process

We dropped the "all-in-one analytics platform" line and led with the operator's real pain: the report nobody trusts, the spend nobody can explain. Each page opened on one specific job-to-be-done and proved it with real product views and benchmark numbers, not stock dashboards. The Arabic creative was localized, not translated — written for how GCC ops and finance leaders actually evaluate tooling.

results

CAC fell 41% in six months while pipeline grew rather than shrank — marketing-sourced pipeline rose 2.3x on a flat budget. Rebuilt pages converted 63% better, and AI workflows cut content cycle time by 71%, freeing the team to test instead of produce. For the first time, finance and marketing agreed on the numbers: every channel had a defensible cost-to-revenue line.

lessons

AI didn't replace the marketers — it removed the busywork between a good idea and a live test, and that speed is where the CAC savings actually came from. The bigger unlock was attribution: once spend mapped cleanly to revenue, killing the worst-performing third of campaigns was an easy, evidence-backed call. Fix measurement before you scale spend, or you just lose money faster.
