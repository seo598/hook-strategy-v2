the problem

Nova Finance had built serious infrastructure — multi-currency payments and treasury automation that GCC finance teams genuinely needed — but you couldn't tell that from the outside. The brand read like a side project, the site explained the technology instead of the buyer's problem, and acquisition rested on a single founder-led channel. CAC climbed every quarter, and pipeline had been flat for three straight months.

strategy

We treated this as a positioning problem first and a media problem second. We repositioned Nova around the CFO's real pain — cash visibility and reconciliation across Bahrain, Saudi and the wider GCC — and built a full-funnel system where brand, site and paid media carried one message instead of three. The bet was simple: make the product legible to the right buyer, then put budget only behind the proof that the new narrative converts.

execution

We ran rebrand, site rebuild, paid media and content as four parallel workstreams over ten weeks — not a sequential relay. The new site shipped on a fresh design system with conversion-led pages per ICP segment, Arabic and English variants for KSA, and analytics wired into HubSpot so we could attribute spend to qualified opportunities, not clicks. Paid launched in week six against a tight test matrix; we cut underperforming audiences weekly and reinvested in the treasury-pain creative that kept winning.

creative process

The idea was "See every dirham before it moves" — turning abstract treasury software into the felt experience of a finance lead who finally has control. We dropped the stock-fintech gradients for a numbers-forward visual language: real dashboards, real currency flows, plain-language proof points. Bilingual ad sets held the same tone in Arabic and English, so the brand read native in Riyadh and Manama rather than translated.

results

Across two quarters, qualified pipeline grew 312% and CAC fell 41%, while demo-request conversion rose 2.7x on the rebuilt site. The bigger shift: pipeline stopped depending on the founder's calendar. Non-brand search and LinkedIn now feed a predictable, compounding flow of qualified treasury buyers across Bahrain and KSA. Nova went from invisible to the obvious choice in the conversations it used to lose by default.

lessons

Distribution can't fix positioning. The 41% CAC drop came because we fixed what Nova said before we scaled what it spent. Running rebrand, site and media as parallel tracks instead of a sequence bought back weeks of momentum — and wiring attribution to pipeline, not clicks, on day one is what let us cut losing audiences fast and reinvest with confidence.
