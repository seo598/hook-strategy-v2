the problem

Souq Eats was spending hard across paid social and search while ROAS sat at 1.1x and regional incumbents undercut them on both price and recall. The account had sprawled into 40-plus overlapping ad sets bidding against each other for the same audiences, and the creative was English-first in markets where most orders are placed in Arabic. Cost per order climbed every month, with no clean line between spend and orders.

strategy

We fixed structure first, creative second. Collapse the bloated account into a clean prospecting-to-retargeting funnel, rebuild measurement so every dinar mapped to a tracked order, then build creative natively in Arabic and English rather than translating English ads after the fact. Search came in to capture the high-intent "order food near me" demand the competition was quietly owning.

execution

We consolidated 40-plus ad sets into six campaigns with broad, de-duplicated audiences and Advantage+ Shopping for prospecting, then repaired the Conversions API so server-side order events flowed cleanly back to Meta and Google. We rebuilt Google Ads around three jobs: branded defense, generic intent and competitor conquest, with geo-bid adjustments down to the neighborhood in Manama, Riyadh and Jeddah. Budget moved weekly toward the audiences and dayparts that converted; underperformers were cut without ceremony.

creative process

We ran two native tracks, Arabic-first and English-first, instead of copy that reads like a translation. The Arabic track leaned into local dishes, Khaleeji food cues and weekend-night ordering; the English track hit expat and younger urban audiences with speed-and-value hooks. Every concept was shot vertical for the feed with the offer on screen in the first two seconds, on a tight test-and-kill cadence so winners scaled and losers died fast.

results

In 60 days blended ROAS climbed from 1.1x to 3.2x, cost per order fell 41%, and tracked orders from paid rose 68% on roughly flat spend. The Arabic-first track beat English by 2.4x on click-through and carried most of the efficient scale, while the rebuilt search structure recaptured high-intent demand that had been leaking to competitors. Souq Eats left the sprint with a paid account they could scale, not firefight.

lessons

In the GCC, Arabic-first creative is the performance lever, not a nice-to-have. Translated English ads leave money on the table every time. Clean account structure and server-side measurement usually unlock more ROAS than chasing new audiences, because you can't optimize spend you can't trace. And against well-funded local incumbents, owning high-intent search is cheaper insurance than out-shouting them in the feed.
