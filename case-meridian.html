the problem

Meridian Health runs a network of clinics across Bahrain and the Eastern Province, but in search it barely existed. For high-intent terms like "dermatologist in Bahrain" and "best pediatric clinic Manama," competitors and aggregator directories owned page one while Meridian sat on page four or worse. And when people asked ChatGPT or Perplexity "where should I go for a skin check in Bahrain," the brand never came up — invisible in exactly the moment a patient was choosing where to book.

strategy

We treated Google and AI answer engines as one funnel, not two. The plan: fix the technical foundation so Google could crawl and trust the site, build real topical authority around each specialty so Meridian read as the obvious expert, then structure the content to be quotable by LLMs through clean markup, schema, and citable facts. The thesis was simple — earn the ranking on Google and the citation inside AI answers follows, because both reward the same thing: clear, trustworthy, well-structured expertise.

execution

We rebuilt the site architecture around specialty and location hubs, cut crawl waste, fixed Core Web Vitals, and repaired a broken hreflang setup that was pitting the Arabic and English versions against each other. We shipped MedicalClinic, Physician, and FAQPage schema across every location and doctor profile, then built a topical-authority content engine — symptom guides, treatment explainers, and "which specialist do I need" pages — each reviewed by Meridian's clinicians for E-E-A-T. For GEO/AEO we restructured key pages into direct-answer formats: concise definitions, comparison tables, and FAQ blocks that LLMs lift cleanly. Then we tracked brand mentions in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and AI Overviews week over week.

creative process

The editorial voice was calm, plain clinical authority — answering the worried 11pm "should I see a doctor about this" question without jargon or scare tactics. We wrote bilingual content where it mattered, in Arabic that reads as native rather than translated, and gave every doctor a real profile: credentials, languages spoken, and the conditions they treat. The throughline — be the source a careful patient, and an AI, would trust enough to cite by name.

results

Within nine months Meridian ranked #1 on Google for its core specialty-plus-location terms across Bahrain and the Eastern Province, and started getting named directly inside ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews when patients asked where to go. Organic search traffic rose 312% and organic appointment leads climbed 168% — from 38 a month to 102 — while the AI-citation channel grew from zero to a steady ~47 brand mentions a month. The aggregator directories that used to outrank them now sit below them, and the site has become the answer patients and machines reach for first.

lessons

GEO/AEO is not a separate discipline bolted onto SEO. The same structural clarity, schema, and credible expertise that wins Google rankings is exactly what gets you quoted by LLMs. In healthcare, clinician review of every page wasn't a compliance checkbox — it was the trust signal that made both Google and AI engines comfortable citing us. And in the GCC, bilingual content has to be written native in each language; translation alone leaves rankings and citations on the table.
